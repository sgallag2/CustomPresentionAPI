The 10 Principles of Design

1. Contrast

Contrast in web design plays with the juxtaposition of opposing elements...
...through the use of different colors, tones, lines and shapes to produce emphasis.

2. Emphasis

Emphasis creates dominant and nondominant elements to draw attention on a single area. Scroll over the pictures to see how emphasis in web design works.

3. Balance

Balance in web design uses symmetry and repetition to balance a web design. Notice how these babies line up well together, keeping the page in balance.

4. Unity/Harmony

Producing unity or harmony in web design refers to the pleasing arrangement of all the parts within a composition. Below, for example, all of the elements are positioned around a central point.

5. Pattern

Offers a way to keep your design organized while also providing a really nice sense of harmony through the use of repeating elements.

6. Movement

Movement is the suggestion of action or direction

7. Rhythm and Repetition

Rhythm and Repetition occurs in design when one or more elements are repeated often enough to create a visual rhythm.

Rhythm and repetition is vital to establishing consistency: it organizes information coherently, guides the audience, brings together the elements of a design, and emphasizes space.

8. Proportion

Proportion is the visual relationship between two or more things in terms of size, number or degree.

Differing proportions within a design creates a system of hierarchy, communicating to viewers which elements are most important.

Right now, this design is poorly proportioned. Users cannot quickly pick up on what's most important. Click the plus icon to see how proportion works in web design.

Google Maps API

A Javascript program that enables web designers to add maps to their websites with customized information and restrictions. For example, with Google Maps API, designers can add maps that centralize on a specific location, change the depth of focus of the map, and click defined points for more information about the map’s content. Go to the next slide to see an example.

This map shows mass shootings that occurred in the DMV area in 2015. The map is styled to reflect the Maryland colors. As the map shows, the majority of shootings in the area were centered around Baltimore city and DC. A mass shooting, in this context, is defined as a shooting in which four or more people are killed or injured. Green markers indicate incidents with no fatalities, yellow markers resulted in one death, and red markers more than one person died. Source: shootingtracker.com

D3.js

Data-Driven Documents (Hence the D3/D3.js) is a Javascript library for creating dynamic, interactive data visualizations in a web browser. D3 is built on top of common web standards like HTML, HTML5, CSS, and CSS3. Click the next arrow to see an example of D3.js visualization.

This chart, made using D3, shows the number of total shooting homicides in Baltimore by year. As you can see, this number has spiked in 2015. Hover over each bar. Source: The Baltimore Sun
